02Your First Login

You received a printed sheet with your email and temporary password from the Secretary. Follow these steps:

1

Open the app

Go to the USC Attendance URL on your phone's browser (Chrome on Android, Safari on iPhone).

Tip: Add to Home Screen for quick access. Tap the browser menu → "Add to Home Screen" → name it "USC Attendance".
2

Enter your credentials

Type your email and temporary password exactly as printed. Passwords are case-sensitive.

3

Change your password

You'll be forced to change your password immediately. Choose something you'll remember — you'll use it every time you log in.

Must be at least 8 characters. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for security.
4

You're in!

After changing your password, you'll land on the dashboard. You're ready to go.

Forgot your password?

Tap "Forgot Password" on the login screen. Enter your email and you'll receive a reset link. No need to ask the Secretary.

04Checking In to an Event

When you arrive at an event venue, you can check in using your phone's GPS. The system verifies you're physically within the event's radius.

1

Open the app at the venue

Make sure you're physically at the event location. The app uses your phone's GPS to verify your position.

2

Tap "Check In for Event"

Select the event from the list (or it auto-selects if there's only one active event).

3

Allow location access

Your browser will ask for location permission. Tap "Allow" — the app needs this to verify you're at the venue.

If you deny location access, you can't self check-in. Ask staff to do a manual override or use the QR method instead.
4

Confirm check-in

If you're within the venue radius (usually 50–75 meters), you'll see a green "Within venue radius" status. Tap "Check In" to confirm.

Too far from venue?

If you see a red distance indicator, you're outside the allowed radius. Walk closer to the venue and try again. If you're having GPS issues, ask staff to do a manual override or use QR check-in.

05QR Code Check-In

If GPS check-in isn't working (bad signal, in-app browser issues), staff can scan your QR code instead. This is faster and doesn't require location access.

1

Open your QR code

From the dashboard, tap "Show QR Code" (or go to the QR page). Select the event you're checking in to.

2

Show it to staff

A QR code appears on your screen. Hold it up for the staff member with the scanner. The code is valid for 60 seconds and auto-refreshes.

3

Done!

Staff scans it, and your attendance is recorded instantly. You'll see a confirmation on both screens.

Why does the QR expire?

Your QR code contains a signed token that's only valid for 60 seconds. This prevents screenshot sharing — each code is unique to you, your event, and that specific moment. There's nothing to print, save, or share.

06Office Duty Check-In

If you're on the weekly duty schedule, check in each day you're assigned to the office. This is separate from event attendance.

1

Check if you have duty today

Your dashboard shows today's duty schedule. If you're assigned, you'll see your time slot (e.g., 08:00–17:00).

2

Tap "Check in for duty"

When you arrive at the office during your scheduled hours, open the app and tap the duty check-in button.

3

Confirm

Your duty attendance is recorded. You can only check in once per day — the button disappears after successful check-in.

No duty today?

If your dashboard says "No office duty today," you're not scheduled. You can still check in to events if any are happening.

07Roles & Access

Not everyone sees the same thing. Your role determines what you can do in the system.

Role Who Can Do
super_admin President Everything: manage officers, create events, view all attendance, scan QR, manual override
staff VP, Secretary, Auditor, Spokesperson, Senate Press Create events, view attendance, scan QR, manual override for other officers
client Senators Check in to events (GPS/QR), check in for duty, view own attendance
Staff note

You can do manual overrides for other officers (e.g., if their phone died). But you cannot manually record your own attendance — use self check-in or QR for yourself. This is by design to keep the audit trail honest.

08Frequently Asked Questions

What if my phone's GPS is inaccurate?

Try moving to an open area (away from buildings). If GPS still doesn't work, use QR check-in — show your code to staff and they'll scan it.

What if I'm using the app inside Facebook or Messenger?

In-app browsers (Facebook, Messenger, Viber) often block location and camera access. Open the app in your phone's default browser instead (Chrome on Android, Safari on iPhone). Better yet, add it to your home screen.

Can I check in from home?

No. The system verifies your GPS location. You must be within the venue's radius (usually 50–75 meters) to self check-in. QR check-in requires you to be physically present with staff.

What if I forget my password?

Tap "Forgot Password" on the login screen. Enter your email and you'll receive a reset link. Check your inbox (and spam folder).

Can I check in to an event after it's started?

Yes, as long as the event is still active. There's no time limit for check-in — the system only cares that you were physically present.

What if I have duty AND an event on the same day?

Check in for both separately. Duty check-in is for your office hours; event check-in is for the specific event. They're tracked independently.

Why do I see "Already checked in"?

You can only check in once per event (or once per day for duty). If you see this message, your attendance is already recorded.

Does the app work offline?

Yes. If you lose connection, your check-in is queued locally and syncs automatically when you're back online. You'll see a "queued" indicator until it syncs.
